Advanced Recombinant Transformer: Modeling Instrument-Level Loop Compatibility with Self-Supervised Acoustic Embeddings

Abstract Vertical loop compatibility — deciding whether two instrument loops can be layered in a musically coherent way — has seen limited success, primarily due to the absence of a large-scale, high-fidelity, labeled dataset. We introduce ART, which renders studio-quality stems from multi-instrument MIDI to create aligned positive pairs, and mines matched-condition hard negatives using a frozen DRT scorer with calibrated thresholds based on a blinded musician audit. For representation, we replace log-mel features with frozen embeddings from MERT that capture timbral nuances and provide cues for higher-level musical structure. An order-invariant head then scores pairwise compatibility. Experimental results show a 10.1% improvement in compatibility classification over the previous state-of-the-art. Extensive subjective tests (N = 107), on production-grade human composed loops, find seamlessness and creativity ratings comparable to human performance.
